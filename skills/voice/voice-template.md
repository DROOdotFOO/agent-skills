# Voice Profile Template

Copy this structure when adding a new writer to `voices/`.

```markdown
---
impact: medium
impactDescription: Voice profile for <writer name>
tags: writing, voice, <writer-name>
---

# <Writer Name>

Source: <primary URL or publication>
Studied from: <list of specific essays/chapters read>

## The core move

One sentence: what does this writer do that most writers don't?

## Sentence rhythm

- Average sentence length tendency (short/medium/long/varied)
- How they vary rhythm (do they alternate? build? stay uniform?)
- Signature sentence structures (e.g., "X. Y. But Z." or long
  comma-heavy clauses that circle back)

## Paragraph structure

- Typical paragraph length (sentences)
- How they open paragraphs (topic sentence? scene? question? mid-thought?)
- How they close paragraphs (conclusion? cliffhanger? trailing off?)
- Do they vary paragraph length dramatically or keep it uniform?

## Rhetorical moves

List 3-7 specific moves this writer makes. Not vague qualities
("clear", "engaging") but concrete techniques you can replicate:

- e.g., "Defines terms mid-essay as gifts to the reader, not jargon setup"
- e.g., "Lets you see the thinking change direction mid-paragraph"
- e.g., "Uses parentheticals for self-aware humor about their own position"

## Emotional register

- How do they handle uncertainty? (hide it? display it? sit with it?)
- How do they handle strong claims? (hedge? assert? assert then qualify?)
- Where does humor show up? (asides? metaphors? self-deprecation? nowhere?)
- How do they handle being wrong or not knowing?

## What to borrow

The 3-5 most transferable moves -- things that improve most prose
regardless of topic:

1. ...
2. ...
3. ...

## What NOT to borrow

Every writer has tics or habits that don't transfer well. List them
so the blender knows what to skip:

- e.g., "Tendency to over-qualify every claim -- in technical writing
  this becomes hedging soup"
- e.g., "Relies on shared cultural references that don't land outside
  their audience"

## Calibration anchors

3-5 short passages (2-4 sentences each) that demonstrate the writer's
voice at its most distinctive. These are reference points -- when
reviewing a draft, compare against these to check if the borrowed
moves are landing.

### Anchor 1: [label]

> passage

### Anchor 2: [label]

> passage

### Anchor 3: [label]

> passage
```

## Quality checks

Before adding a voice file, verify:

- [ ] You read at least 3 different pieces by this writer (not just one essay)
- [ ] "The core move" is specific enough that it distinguishes this writer from others
- [ ] "Rhetorical moves" are concrete and actionable, not adjectives
- [ ] "What NOT to borrow" has at least 2 entries (every writer has anti-patterns)
- [ ] Calibration anchors are real quotes, not paraphrases
- [ ] The profile works for blending -- moves don't assume you're writing about the same topics
